# CLS datasets at the UK Data Service

## Survey overview

The Community Life Survey (CLS) is a household survey of adults aged 16+ in England, covering volunteering, charitable giving, views about the local area, community cohesion, civic engagement, subjective wellbeing and loneliness. Commissioned by the Cabinet Office in 2012; the Department for Culture, Media and Sport (DCMS) has published results from 2016-17 onwards. Fieldwork was conducted face-to-face in 2012/13, with push-to-web (online/paper) tested from 2013/14 and used exclusively from 2016/17. The survey was run by Verian (formerly Kantar Public) on behalf of DCMS.

In 2023/24 and 2024/25, DCMS partnered with the Ministry of Housing, Communities and Local Government (MHCLG) to boost the sample to support estimates at local authority level. Fieldwork in both years ran over two quarters (Oct-Dec and Jan-Mar). There is no 2022/23 annual dataset; fieldwork paused while the boosted design was developed.

CLS fieldwork ended on 30 March 2025. Following a 2024 consultation, DCMS issued a tender for a combined social survey (CLS and Participation Survey content) commencing October 2025, with CLS-content data expected at local authority level in partnership with MHCLG. See the "Update to the Community Life Survey" section of the GOV.UK collection page.

Secure Access editions contain the detailed variables not included in safeguarded (EUL) releases, e.g. lower-level geography consistent with the LA-level boost. Access is via UKDS SecureLab only; data cannot be downloaded. Check the study's "Access data" tab in the catalogue for the definitive variable-level differences from the EUL edition.

## Variable-level search

The UKDS catalogue supports variable-level search across studies: https://datacatalogue.ukdataservice.ac.uk/ (Variables search). Useful for checking which variables appear in EUL versus Secure Access editions when specifying synthetic data.

## Data citation

Outputs released from SecureLab must include the full data citation with DOI. The citation for each study appears on its catalogue page and in the 'Study Information' files in the SecureLab project area. Format for SN 9515: Department for Culture, Media and Sport, Verian Group. (2026). Community Life Survey, 2024-2025: Secure Access. [data collection]. UK Data Service. SN: 9515, DOI: http://doi.org/10.5255/UKDA-SN-9515-1 (verify exact citation on the catalogue page).
